For more than 25 years, Dr. Roysdon has worked in engineering and applied mathematics, solving real-world problems.  His career spans academia, private industry and federal government service.  Dr. Roysdon presently leads a cutting-edge artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning (ML) research team at a Fortune 500 company where he is a Vice President, AI/ML Chief Scientist, and a Technical Fellow.

%He learned how to do research and innovate while on a ten-person research and development team that -- in the span of nine years -- designed, built and fielded six different fleets of military subsonic and supersonic unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs).  He is the co-designer of five turbojet powered UAVs, sole designer of two UAVs, sole author of seven autopilots, four navigation systems, and three camera stabilization systems.  Recently, his research has focused on applied mathematics, specifically artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning (ML), applied to cyber security, intelligence analysis and cryptography.  Dr. Roysdon presently leads a cutting-edge AI/ML research team at a Fortune 500 company where he is the AI/ML Chief Scientist, Vice President, and a Technical Fellow.

Dr. Roysdon earned a Ph.D. Electrical Engineering (focus in applied mathematics & probability theory), from the University of California, as well as an M.S. in Aeronautical Engineering, M.S. in Electrical Engineering, M.S. in Mechanical Engineering, and B.S. in both Aeronautical & Mechanical Engineering. He is a Senior Member of AIAA, Senior Member of IEEE, and served on the AIAA Aircraft Design Technical Committee for 15 years.

Dr. Roysdon has published many peer-reviewed [scientific articles](https://github.com/pfroysdon/publications/tree/main/Papers), [five textbooks](http://www.roysdonfibonaccipress.com/), and [technical notes](https://github.com/pfroysdon/publications/tree/main/Math_Notes) in AI/ML, optimal state estimation, aircraft design, as well as guidance, navigation and control.

His outside interests include [watchmaking](https://www.roysdonwatchco.com/) and [flying aerobatics](https://youtu.be/8kgQ3hz9YNg) in a Pitts S2C.
