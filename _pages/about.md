For more than 25 years, Dr. Roysdon has worked in engineering and applied mathematics, solving real-world problems.  His career spans academia, private industry and federal government.  He learned how to do research and innovate while on a ten-person research and development team that -- in the span of nine years -- designed, built and fielded six different fleets of military subsonic and supersonic unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs).  He is the co-designer of five turbojet powered UAVs, sole designer of two UAVs, sole author of seven autopilots, four navigation systems, and three camera stabilization systems.  Recently, his research has focused on applied mathematics, specifically artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning (ML), applied to cyber security, intelligence analysis and cryptography.  Dr. Roysdon presently leads a cutting-edge AI/ML research team at a Fortune 500 company where he is the AI/ML Chief Scientist, Vice President, and a Technical Fellow.

Dr. Roysdon earned a Ph.D. Electrical Engineering (focus in applied mathematics & probability theory), from the University of California, as well as an M.S. in Aeronautical Engineering, M.S. in Electrical Engineering, M.S. in Mechanical Engineering, and B.S. in both Aeronautical & Mechanical Engineering.

Dr. Roysdon has published many [scientific articles](https://github.com/pfroysdon/publications/tree/main/Papers),  [technical notes](https://github.com/pfroysdon/publications/tree/main/Math_Notes) and [five textbooks](http://www.roysdonfibonaccipress.com/) in AI/ML and optimal state estimation.  He has also posted [analysis and simulation software](https://github.com/pfroysdon/projects) for autopilots, GPS-INS navigation systems, and 6-Degrees-of-Freedom flight simulators (and many more).

When he is not working on [math](http://www.roysdonfibonaccipress.com/), or [watchmaking](https://www.roysdonwatchco.com/), you might see him in the skies [flying aerobatics in a Pitts S2C](https://youtu.be/8kgQ3hz9YNg).
