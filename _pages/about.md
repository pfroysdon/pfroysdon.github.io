*"In everything, commit yourself to excellence and integrity." - P. F. Roysdon*

With over 25 years of experience in engineering and applied mathematics, Dr. Roysdon is a highly competent real-world problem solver. His career has traversed academia, defense contracting, and federal government service. He is a Leidos Vice President, AI Chief Scientist, Technical Fellow, and Master-level Solution Architect at the Leidos Office of Technology, providing AI technical leadership. Dr. Roysdon also imparts his knowledge as an adjunct professor at the National Intelligence University.

Dr. Roysdon’s academic journey (while working full-time) is a testament to his unwavering dedication and deep passion for many fields. He holds a Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering from the University of California, with a dual focus on applied mathematics and probability theory. His educational achievements also include an M.S. in Aeronautical Engineering, an M.S. in Electrical Engineering, an M.S. in Mechanical Engineering, and a B.S. in both Aeronautical and Mechanical Engineering. He has also completed graduate-level studies in theoretical physics and constitutional law with a focus on AI. He is a Senior Member of IEEE and a Senior Member of AIAA, where he served on the AIAA Aircraft Design Technical Committee for 15 years.

Dr. Roysdon has received numerous awards for innovations in artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning (ML) applied to problems in the Intelligence Community, the Department of Defense, and the private sector, e.g., the National Security Agency (NSA) “Inventor Award,” the NSA “Director’s Reserve Award,” the Office of the Director of National Intelligence “Director’s Award,” the Leidos “Innovation in R&D Award,” and twice received the Leidos “CTO Award.”

Dr. Roysdon has six patents pending and has authored many peer-reviewed [textbooks](http://www.roysdonfibonaccipress.com/), [scientific articles](https://github.com/pfroysdon/publications/tree/main/Papers), and [technical notes](https://github.com/pfroysdon/publications/tree/main/Tech_Notes).  His [personal research](https://pfroysdon.github.io/projects/) includes theoretical and applied AI/ML, Offensive and Defensive Cyber, Cryptanalysis, atomic and Nuclear Physics, Aircraft Design, Guidance, Navigation, and control (GNC), and RF signal analysis.

His other interests include [flying](https://youtu.be/8kgQ3hz9YNg) and [watchmaking](https://www.roysdonwatchco.com/).
